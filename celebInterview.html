CHAT WITH SONAM KAPPOOR

97 LIKES

“ Facing a slew of deadlines and the gloom that so often accompanies summer's end, I was suddenly desperate to escape the demands of daily life. „

  • Q.

    AS ZOYA IN RAANJHANAA YOU GAVE AN IMPRESSIVE PERFORMANCE.

  • Zoya is a girl with shades. She is a student at the JNU (Jawaharlal Nehru University) and also has an attitude. I visited the campus, interacted with the students and did street plays to get a feel of the character. Being a Punjabi, I have an accent. But to play Zoya, a Muslim, I needed to develop a command over Hindi and Urdu.

  • Q.

    HOW WAS IT WORKING WITH DHANUSH?

  • He’s a good actor and humble too. I have love and respect for him. (Laughs) We are good friends but he is a little afraid of me.

  • Q.

    WHY?

  • Not only he but the entire unit was afraid of me. I behaved like a teacher scolding everyone.

  • Q.

    YOU MUST HAVE DISCUSSED SOUTH INDIAN FILMS WITH HIM...

  • Yes, in the South they wrap up a film really fast – say within 30 to 40 days. While shooting Raanjhanaa, he asked me whether the film was too long. Because in the South they would have shot two films in that time.

  • Q.

    HOW WOULD YOU DEFINE LOVE?

  • What is shown in films is not love but what you saw in Raanjhanaa is definitely love. In films everything is just so perfect, perfect hero, perfect heroine and perfect situation. But in real life nothing is perfect. I believe love changes people.

  • Q.

    DO YOU BELIEVE THAT MARRIAGE DESTROYS LOVE?

  • You can remain in love even after marriage. I don’t believe that love vanishes after marriage. It has been 28 years since my parents (Anil Kapoor and Sunita) have been married. Before that they were dating for 11 years. They’ve been together for the past 39 years! Yet they love each other. Recently, my dad asked me, “Find out which year the film The Exorcist (1973) was released. That was the first time I held your mom’s hand!” He still remembers such small incidents. This is love.
